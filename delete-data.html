Delete some of your Defensive Pedal data

Effective date: 23 April 2026 · Last updated: 23 April 2026

This page explains how to request deletion of specific categories of personal data collected by Defensive Pedal without deleting your whole account. Defensive Pedal is operated by Antifragil SRL ("Antifragil", "we", "us"). Partial deletion is free, requires no justification, and can be requested at any time.

Want to delete your whole account instead? Follow the instructions on our Delete your account page.

What you can delete on your own, inside the app

Some deletions are available directly inside Defensive Pedal and do not require emailing us:

  • Individual trips. Open the app, go to History → Trips, open the trip, and use the delete option on the trip detail screen. The trip and its breadcrumb track are removed from your history and from the community feed (if the trip was shared) within 30 days.

For everything else described below, send us an email following the steps in the next section.

How to request partial data deletion — step by step

  1. Open your email application and start a new message from the email address associated with your Defensive Pedal account (the same address you use to sign in, either directly or via Google).
  2. Address the email to victor@defensivepedal.com.
  3. Use the subject line: Delete some of my Defensive Pedal data.
  4. In the body of the email, clearly list which category or categories you want deleted. You can use plain language, for example: "Please delete all my trip history and breadcrumb tracks, but keep my account and profile."
  5. Send the email.

We will reply within 5 business days to confirm receipt and to verify that the email was sent by the account holder. The requested data will be deleted from our production systems within 30 days of that confirmation. Your account will remain active, and you can keep using the app normally.

Data categories you can ask us to delete

You can request deletion of one, several, or all of the categories below while keeping your account. Each row describes what gets deleted and what effect it has on your use of the app.

Category What gets deleted Effect on the app
Trip history All route records, breadcrumb GPS tracks, distance / duration / elevation metrics, and derived statistics tied to your account. Your Trips screen will be empty. New trips you record afterwards will be stored normally.
Community content you submitted Hazard reports, hazard votes and confirmations, shared trips, likes and reactions, comments, and post-ride feedback. Your contributions are removed from the community feed. Other users' content is unaffected.
Profile picture The photo you uploaded as your profile image. Your profile reverts to a default avatar. You can upload a new photo later.
Display name The display name you set. Your profile reverts to a generic identifier. You can set a new display name later.
Progression data Experience points (XP), streak history, badge unlocks, tier, and leaderboard position. Your progression resets to zero. Future rides will start earning XP and streaks again.
Push notification token The pseudonymous device token used to deliver push notifications. You stop receiving push notifications. You can re-enable them in Settings to register a new token.
App preferences Theme, distance units, default bike type, voice guidance, map POI visibility, notification preferences, quiet hours. Preferences reset to defaults on next sign-in. You can reconfigure them from the Settings screen.
Crash logs linked to your account Diagnostic logs that reference your account identifier. No user-visible effect. Anonymized aggregate statistics may still be retained (see next section).

What we keep (and why)

Some data must remain even after a partial-deletion request, either because it is needed to keep your account operational, because we are legally required to keep it, or because it has been irreversibly anonymized and no longer relates to you.

Category Retention period Reason
Account credentials (email, password hash or OAuth identifier) As long as your account is active Required to let you keep signing in. If you also want these removed, use the account-deletion flow.
Aggregated and anonymized statistics Indefinite Used for safety research and service improvement. Personal identifiers are removed; this data cannot be linked back to you.
Anonymized crash logs and diagnostic data Up to 90 days After this period, crash data is either aggregated or deleted.
Legal, financial, and tax records (if any) Up to 5 years Retained only where required by Romanian law, for example, proof of paid transactions. Not applicable if you never made a payment.
Backup snapshots Up to 30 days after deletion Encrypted database backups may still contain the deleted data until the backup rotation window closes. Backups are not used for any operational purpose and are overwritten on schedule.

Your rights under the GDPR

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, partial data deletion is one way to exercise your right to erasure (Article 17) and your right to restriction of processing (Article 18) under the GDPR. You also have the right to access and correct your data, and to data portability. For details on all your rights and how to exercise them, see our Privacy Policy.

If you believe your data has not been handled in accordance with the GDPR, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Romanian supervisory authority, the National Supervisory Authority for Personal Data Processing (ANSPDCP) — www.dataprotection.ro.
